The Maturity Gap
is our market map.

The gap is real and significant. Western and Northern EU markets have had BIM mandates for up to 15 years. Eastern EU is 5–10 years behind. This creates both a target sequence and a pricing sensitivity gradient: mature markets have the highest volume, deepest understanding of our value proposition, and the highest labour costs driving automation demand.

★★★★
Tier 1 — Target first
UK · Germany
Netherlands · Belgium
BIM mandatory for all public projects. ISO 19650 standard. Labour costs €65–90/hr driving acute automation demand. Renovation and retrofit sectors are mature, scan-to-BIM is standard practice at large firms but remains expensive. Highest concentration of early adopters.
★★★
Tier 2 — Target second
Sweden · Norway
Denmark · Finland
Global BIM pioneers. Denmark mandated in 2007, Norway in 2010. Open BIM (IFC) standard — critical to support. Highest labour costs in Europe (€80–120/hr). Demand for precision is extreme. Smaller market size but premium pricing potential and strong community networks.
★★

Italy · Spain
France · Portugal
Rapidly maturing via EU Recovery Funds. Italy mandated BIM for all public projects >€1M from 2025. Spain and France building national BIM programmes. High volume of heritage and infrastructure retrofit creates strong scan-to-BIM demand. Price sensitivity higher.
★

Hungary · CEE
Poland · Romania
BIM largely limited to large infrastructure and multinational-led commercial projects. Residential and small-scale commercial sectors remain 2D/CAD based. Our home base — valuable for community building and close customer relationships during alpha, but not our primary revenue source in Phase 1.

Sector breakdown within BIM-mature markets
Government/Infrastructure: Near-universal in Tier 1/2 markets. Mandatory procurement. Highest volume per project. Slow sales cycles (6–18 months). Not primary Founding 100 targets.
Commercial: Very high adoption in Tier 1 markets — office, retail, healthcare, education. BIM managers are key buyers. Medium sales cycles (2–6 months). Prime Founding 100 target.
Residential: Growing in Tier 1, nascent in Tier 3/4. Renovation and retrofit projects are the highest-volume scan-to-BIM use case. Individual architect practices are the buyer. Fast sales cycles (<4 weeks). Highest-volume Founding 100 target.
Go-to-market strategy

A three-phase
market entry.

The Founding 100 is not just a financing mechanism — it is our GTM strategy. Each founding member is a reference customer, a community anchor, and a word-of-mouth vector in their local market. The phasing below maps how our founding cohort converts into sustainable growth.

Phase 01
Founding 100
April – October 2026
Direct outreach to 200 hand-picked prospects across Tier 1 markets. Our target: 100 paid seats. Each founding member is personally onboarded by our team. We focus on firms already doing scan-to-BIM manually — the pain is known and our ROI case is immediate.
Phase 02
Community Launch
Q4 2026 – Q2 2027
Full release. Our founding members become reference customers. We publish LinkedIn case studies and establish a conference presence at Digital Construction Week, BIM World Munich, and BIM Show Live. The referral programme activates. Target: 200 additional paying firms.
Phase 03
Market Scale
2027+
Tier 3 markets (Italy, Spain, France) open as mandates bite. We pursue channel partnerships with Leica, Faro, and Trimble reseller networks who already hold scanner customer relationships. Our target: 1,000+ firms. Enterprise contracts with large survey bureaus.
Phase 04
CEE Expansion
2028+
Eastern EU as BIM mandates spread — Hungary, Poland, Romania, Czech Republic. Our home advantage becomes a local distribution asset. Lower price tolerance but lower CAC. We target the government infrastructure pipeline as EU recovery funds flow.
